Zurich

What to See

Zurich boasts a mix of natural beauty and urban sophistication. Key sights include Lake Zurich, the Old Town (Altstadt), Bahnhofstrasse for shopping, the Swiss National Museum, and the Lindenhof district.

A Brief History of the City

Zurich has a history dating back to Roman times. It became a major center during the Middle Ages and played a significant role in the Protestant Reformation. Today, Zurich is a global financial hub while retaining its historical charm.

Top 10 Attractions

  1. Lake Zurich
  2. Old Town (Altstadt)
  3. Bahnhofstrasse
  4. Swiss National Museum
  5. Lindenhof District
  6. Uetliberg Mountain
  7. Kunsthaus Zurich
  8. Grossmünster
  9. Fraumünster Church
  10. Zurich Zoo

What to Bring

  • Comfortable walking shoes
  • Weather-appropriate clothing
  • Camera or smartphone for photos
  • Guidebook or map
  • Travel adapter (Type C or Type J socket)
  • Reusable water bottle
  • Personal medications
  • Umbrella or raincoat
  • Light jacket for cooler evenings
  • Basic German phrases

Currency

The currency in Zurich is the Swiss Franc (CHF).

Best Time to Visit

Spring (April to June) and Autumn (September to October) offer pleasant weather and beautiful landscapes. Summer is ideal for outdoor activities by the lake, while winter is perfect for enjoying Zurich's festive holiday markets.

Fun Facts

  • Zurich is consistently ranked as one of the world's most livable cities.
  • The Bahnhofstrasse in Zurich is one of the most expensive shopping streets globally.
  • Zurich is home to the famous luxury watch brands, including Rolex and Patek Philippe.
  • The Grossmünster church is legendary for its connection to the Protestant Reformation.
  • Zurich has over 50 lakes within a 100 km radius, offering numerous recreational opportunities.
