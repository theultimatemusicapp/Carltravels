Corfu

History

Corfu, a Greek island in the Ionian Sea, has a history influenced by various civilizations, including the Romans, Byzantines, Venetians, and British. Its strategic location made it a significant naval base and cultural melting pot over the centuries.

Population

As of 2023, Corfu has a population of approximately 100,000 people, with a vibrant mix of locals and seasonal tourists.

Notable Attractions

  • Achilleion Palace
  • Corfu Old Town
  • Paleokastritsa Monastery
  • Kanoni Peninsula
  • Liston Promenade

Best Time to Visit

The best time to visit Corfu is during the late spring (May and June) and early autumn (September and October) when the weather is warm and the crowds are thinner.

Prices Overview

Corfu offers a range of accommodation options. Budget travelers can find hostels and guesthouses starting at €20 per night, while mid-range hotels range from €60-€150 per night. Dining out is affordable, with meals costing between €10 and €25.

Fun Facts

  • Corfu was the birthplace of the famous composer Vangelis.
  • The island is home to the rare black squirrels, native only to Corfu.
  • Corfu Old Town is a UNESCO World Heritage Site recognized for its unique Venetian architecture.
