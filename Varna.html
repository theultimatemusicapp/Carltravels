Varna

What to See

Varna, known as the "Sea Capital of Bulgaria," offers stunning beaches, historic sites, and a vibrant cultural scene. Key attractions include the Varna Archaeological Museum, Sea Garden, Aladzha Monastery, Varna Cathedral, and the Dolphinarium.

A Brief History of the City

Varna has a history that spans over 3,000 years. It was an important center during the Thracian and Roman periods. The city has evolved into a modern metropolis while preserving its rich historical and cultural heritage.

Top 10 Attractions

  1. Varna Archaeological Museum
  2. Sea Garden (Morska Gradina)
  3. Aladzha Monastery
  4. Varna Cathedral
  5. Dolphinarium
  6. Roman Baths
  7. Varna Aquarium
  8. Asparuhovo Riviera
  9. Stone Forest (Pobiti Kamani)
  10. Kamenitza Winery

What to Bring

  • Comfortable walking shoes
  • Beachwear and sunscreen (if visiting beaches)
  • Weather-appropriate clothing
  • Camera or smartphone for photos
  • Guidebook or map
  • Travel adapter (Type F socket)
  • Reusable water bottle
  • Personal medications
  • Light jacket for cooler evenings
  • Basic Bulgarian phrases

Currency

The currency in Varna (Bulgaria) is the Bulgarian Lev (BGN).

Best Time to Visit

Spring (April to June) and Autumn (September to October) offer pleasant weather and fewer tourists. Summer is ideal for beach activities and enjoying outdoor festivals, while winter provides a quieter atmosphere with mild temperatures.

Fun Facts

  • Varna is home to the oldest gold treasure in the world, dating back to 4,600 BC.
  • The Sea Garden is one of the largest and oldest public parks in Bulgaria.
  • Varna hosts the annual Varna Summer International Music Festival.
  • The Aladzha Monastery is a unique rock-cut monastery with fascinating frescoes.
  • Varna's Dolphinarium is a popular attraction for both locals and tourists.
