Stonehenge

What to See

Stonehenge is one of the world's most famous prehistoric monuments. Visitors can explore the stone circle, visitor center exhibits, and the surrounding landscape, which is steeped in mystery and legend.

A Brief History of the City

Stonehenge was constructed between 3000 BC and 2000 BC. Its exact purpose remains unknown, but theories suggest it was used for religious ceremonies, astronomical observations, or as a burial site.

  • Travel adapter (Type G socket)

  • Personal medications
  • Sun protection (sunscreen, hat, sunglasses)

Best Time to Visit

Spring (April to June) and Autumn (September to October) offer pleasant weather and fewer crowds. Visiting during the summer solstice provides a unique experience with traditional ceremonies, while winter visits are quieter but still awe-inspiring.

Fun Facts

  • Stonehenge consists of around 30 standing stones, each weighing up to 25 tons.
  • The stones were transported from Wales, over 200 kilometers away.
  • Stonehenge aligns with the sunrise during the summer solstice and the sunset during the winter solstice.
  • It is estimated that over 1,000 people were involved in the construction of Stonehenge.
  • Stonehenge remains one of the most enduring mysteries of ancient engineering and architecture.
