Marseille

History

Marseille, located on the southeastern coast of France, is one of the oldest cities in Europe, founded around 600 BC by Greek settlers. It has been a crucial port and trading hub throughout history, blending diverse cultures and traditions.

Population

As of 2023, Marseille has a population of approximately 870,000 people, making it the second-largest city in France.

Notable Attractions

  • Old Port (Vieux-Port)
  • Notre-Dame de la Garde Basilica
  • Calanques National Park
  • MuCEM (Museum of European and Mediterranean Civilizations)
  • Château d'If

Best Time to Visit

The best time to visit Marseille is during the late spring (May and June) and early autumn (September and October) when the weather is warm and sunny, perfect for beach activities and exploring the city.

Prices Overview

Marseille offers a variety of accommodation options. Budget travelers can find hostels starting at €20 per night, while mid-range hotels range from €60-€150 per night. Dining out ranges from €15 for casual meals to €40+ at fine dining restaurants.

Fun Facts

  • Marseille is home to the famous soap made from olive oil, known as Savon de Marseille.
  • The city boasts a vibrant street art scene, with murals and installations throughout urban areas.
  • Marseille's Old Port has been a trading center for over 2,600 years.
