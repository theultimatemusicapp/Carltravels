Lisbon

History

Lisbon, the capital of Portugal, is one of the oldest cities in Europe, with a history stretching back over 2,000 years. It has been a major trading hub, especially during the Age of Discovery when explorers set sail from its shores to discover new lands.

Population

As of 2023, Lisbon has a population of approximately 550,000 people, with a metropolitan area housing over 2.8 million residents.

Notable Attractions

  • Belém Tower
  • Jerónimos Monastery
  • São Jorge Castle
  • Alfama District
  • Lisbon Oceanarium

Best Time to Visit

The best time to visit Lisbon is during the spring (March to May) and fall (September to November) when the weather is warm but not too hot, and the city is less crowded.

Prices Overview

Lisbon offers a range of accommodation options. Budget travelers can find hostels starting at €15 per night, while mid-range hotels range from €60-€150 per night. Dining out is affordable, with meals costing between €10 and €30.

Fun Facts

  • Lisbon is known for its iconic trams, especially the historic Tram 28.
  • The city is built on seven hills, offering stunning panoramic views.
  • Lisbon was almost completely destroyed by the devastating earthquake of 1755.
