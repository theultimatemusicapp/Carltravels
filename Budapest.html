Budapest

What to See

Budapest is renowned for its stunning architecture, beautiful landscapes, and vibrant cultural scene. Key attractions include the Hungarian Parliament Building, Buda Castle, Chain Bridge, Thermal Baths (Gellért and Széchenyi), St. Stephen's Basilica, and the scenic Danube River promenade.

A Brief History of the City

Budapest, the capital of Hungary, was formed in 1872 by the unification of Buda, Pest, and Óbuda. The city has a rich history influenced by Roman, Ottoman, and Austro-Hungarian empires. It has been a cultural and economic hub in Central Europe for centuries, known for its contributions to art, music, and architecture.

What to Bring

  • Comfortable walking shoes
  • Weather-appropriate clothing (Budapest weather can be unpredictable)
  • Camera or smartphone for photos
  • Guidebook or map
  • Travel adapter (Type C or F socket)
  • Reusable water bottle
  • Personal medications
  • Sun protection (sunscreen, hat, sunglasses)
  • Light jacket for cooler evenings
  • Basic Hungarian phrases

Most Famous Food

Lángos: A traditional Hungarian street food consisting of deep-fried dough, typically topped with garlic, sour cream, and cheese. It's a must-try when visiting Budapest.

Currency

The currency in Budapest (Hungary) is the Hungarian Forint (HUF).

Best Time to Visit

The best time to visit Budapest is during the spring (April to June) and fall (September to October) when the weather is mild and the city is less crowded. Summer is ideal for enjoying outdoor activities and festivals, while winter is perfect for exploring indoor attractions and experiencing the festive Christmas markets.

Prices Overview

Budapest is known for being affordable. Accommodation ranges from budget hostels at €15 per night to mid-range hotels at €60-€120 per night. Dining out is inexpensive, with meals costing between €8 and €25. Public transportation is efficient and affordable, making it easy to explore the city.

Fun Facts

  • Budapest is home to the world’s first underground metro line, opened in 1896.
  • The city has over 100 thermal springs, making it famous for its spa culture.
  • Budapest hosts one of Europe’s largest Christmas markets, attracting millions of visitors annually.
  • The Hungarian Parliament Building is the third-largest parliament building in the world.
  • Budapest was ranked as one of the most livable cities in the world.
