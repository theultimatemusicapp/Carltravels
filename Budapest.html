Budapest

History

Budapest, the capital of Hungary, was formed in 1872 by the unification of Buda, Pest, and Óbuda. The city has a rich history influenced by Roman, Ottoman, and Austro-Hungarian empires. It has been a cultural and economic hub in Central Europe for centuries.

Population

As of 2023, Budapest has a population of approximately 1.75 million people, making it the largest city in Hungary.

Notable Attractions

  • Hungarian Parliament Building
  • Chain Bridge
  • Thermal Baths (Gellért, Széchenyi)
  • Buda Castle
  • St. Stephen's Basilica

Best Time to Visit

The best time to visit Budapest is during the spring (April to June) and fall (September to October) when the weather is mild and the city is less crowded.

Prices Overview

Budapest is known for being affordable. Accommodation ranges from budget hostels at €15 per night to mid-range hotels at €60-€120 per night. Dining out is inexpensive, with meals costing between €8 and €25.

Fun Facts

  • Budapest is home to the world’s first underground metro line, opened in 1896.
  • The city has over 100 thermal springs, making it famous for its spa culture.
  • Budapest hosts one of Europe’s largest Christmas markets, attracting millions of visitors annually.
