Malta: Your Travel Guide

My Personal Journey in Malta

I’ve had the pleasure of visiting Malta twice. My first trip was back in 2011 with my family. It was especially meaningful because my mum actually lived here in the 1960s—my grandfather was stationed in Malta with the British Army. Walking around the same streets she once knew gave the trip a nostalgic feel.

A few years later, in 2014, I returned to DJ a wedding. That second trip showed me a more modern side of Malta—its lively nightlife and warm hospitality. I love how the island blends English and Maltese; it makes it super easy to get around and communicate. Plus, the weather is generally fantastic, the history is incredible, and let’s not forget the amazing food!

Top 5 Attractions

  1. Valletta – The historic capital with stunning architecture and waterfront views.
  2. Mdina – The Silent City, offering a glimpse into Malta’s medieval past.
  3. Blue Lagoon (Comino) – Crystal-clear waters perfect for swimming and snorkeling.
  4. Ħaġar Qim Temples – Ancient megalithic temples dating back over 5,000 years.
  5. Marsaxlokk Fishing Village – A charming spot to see traditional Maltese boats and enjoy fresh seafood.

What to Bring

  • Comfortable walking shoes
  • Light clothing for the warm Mediterranean climate
  • Sun protection (hat, sunscreen, sunglasses)
  • Reusable water bottle
  • Adapter for Type G sockets

Maltese Food & Culture

One of my favorite things about Malta is the blend of cultures reflected in its cuisine. You’ll find traditional Maltese dishes like pastizzi (flaky pastries filled with ricotta or peas), fresh seafood, and hints of Italian influence. English is widely spoken, so it’s easy to get around, chat with locals, and explore the island’s rich history.

Quick Tips

Currency: Euro (€). Cards are widely accepted.

Language: Maltese & English (both official). English signage is common.

Time Zone: Central European Time (UTC+1), UTC+2 in summer.

Climate: Warm, Mediterranean weather with mild winters.
