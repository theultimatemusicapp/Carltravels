Bratislava

What to See

Bratislava offers a blend of old and new. Highlights include Bratislava Castle, St. Martin's Cathedral, the Old Town, Devin Castle, and the UFO Observation Deck.

A Brief History of the City

Founded in the 9th century, Bratislava has been a significant cultural and political center in Central Europe. It served as the coronation city for Hungarian kings and has a rich blend of architectural styles reflecting its diverse history.

What to Bring

  • Comfortable walking shoes
  • Weather-appropriate clothing
  • Camera or smartphone for photos
  • Guidebook or map
  • Reusable water bottle
  • Travel adapter (Type E socket)
  • Personal medications
  • Light jacket for evenings
  • Umbrella or raincoat
  • Basic Slovak phrases

Currency

The currency in Bratislava is the Euro (€).

Best Time to Visit

Spring (April to June) and Autumn (September to October) are ideal for pleasant weather and fewer tourists. Summer is also popular, especially for outdoor activities.

Fun Facts

  • Bratislava is the only capital city in the world located on both sides of the Danube River.
  • The city has a vibrant café culture with numerous historic and modern cafes.
  • Bratislava was once part of the Austro-Hungarian Empire.
  • The UFO Observation Deck offers panoramic views of the city and the Danube.
  • Bratislava has a thriving arts scene, including theaters, galleries, and live music venues.
