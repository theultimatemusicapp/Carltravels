Berlin

History

Berlin, the capital of Germany, has a rich history that spans centuries. It has been a pivotal center for art, politics, and culture. The city was a focal point during World War II and the Cold War, symbolized by the Berlin Wall that divided East and West Berlin until 1989.

Population

As of 2023, Berlin has a population of approximately 3.8 million people, making it the largest city in Germany and one of the most populous cities in the European Union.

Notable Attractions

  • Brandenburg Gate
  • Berlin Wall Memorial
  • Reichstag Building
  • Museum Island
  • Alexanderplatz

Best Time to Visit

The best time to visit Berlin is during late spring (May and June) and early autumn (September and October) when the weather is mild and pleasant.

Prices Overview

Berlin is relatively affordable compared to other major European capitals. Accommodation ranges from budget hostels at €20 per night to mid-range hotels at €70-€150 per night. Dining out is also reasonably priced, with meals costing between €10 and €25.

Fun Facts

  • Berlin has more bridges than Venice, with over 960 spanning its numerous waterways.
  • The city is home to the world’s largest train station, Berlin Hauptbahnhof.
  • Berlin is known for its vibrant street art and has numerous murals and graffiti throughout the city.
