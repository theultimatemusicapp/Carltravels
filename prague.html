Prague

History

Prague, the capital of the Czech Republic, has a history that dates back over a thousand years. It has been a significant cultural, economic, and political center in Central Europe. The city is renowned for its stunning architecture, including Gothic, Renaissance, and Baroque styles.

Population

As of 2023, Prague has a population of approximately 1.3 million people, making it the largest city in the Czech Republic.

Notable Attractions

  • Charles Bridge
  • Prague Castle
  • Old Town Square and Astronomical Clock
  • St. Vitus Cathedral
  • Wenceslas Square

Best Time to Visit

The ideal times to visit Prague are during the spring (April to June) and fall (September to October) when the weather is mild, and the city is less crowded.

Prices Overview

Prague is known for being relatively affordable. Accommodation ranges from budget hostels at €15 per night to mid-range hotels at €60-€120 per night. Dining out is reasonably priced, with meals costing between €8 and €25.

Fun Facts

  • Prague has the highest density of historical buildings in the world.
  • The city is home to over 30 bridges spanning the Vltava River.
  • Prague was named the European Capital of Culture in 2000.
