Budva

What to See

Budva is renowned for its stunning Adriatic coastline, vibrant nightlife, and well-preserved medieval architecture. Visitors can enjoy pristine beaches, explore the charming Old Town, and partake in a variety of water sports and cultural activities.

A Brief History of the City

With a history spanning over 2,500 years, Budva is one of the oldest settlements on the Adriatic coast. Originally established as a Greek colony, it later became a significant medieval fortress town under Venetian and Ottoman rule. Today, Budva seamlessly blends its rich historical heritage with modern tourism, making it a beloved destination for travelers worldwide.

Top 10 Attractions

  1. Old Town (Stari Grad) - Wander through narrow streets, historic buildings, and ancient city walls in Budva's charming Old Town.
  2. Mogren Beach - A picturesque beach split into two coves, perfect for swimming and sunbathing.
  3. Sveti Nikola Island (Island of the Holy Nicholas) - Accessible by boat, this tranquil island offers beautiful beaches and lush greenery.
  4. Citadel - Explore this fortified complex offering panoramic views of Budva and the Adriatic Sea.
  5. Budva Riviera - A stretch of stunning beaches and resorts along the coast, ideal for relaxation and water activities.
  6. Jaz Beach - One of the largest and most popular beaches in Montenegro, known for its vibrant beach clubs and events.
  7. Serbian Orthodox Church of the Holy Trinity - A beautiful church showcasing traditional Montenegrin architecture.
  8. Budva Marina - A modern marina with luxury yachts, waterfront restaurants, and stunning sea views.
  9. Nightlife in Budva - Experience the town's lively nightlife with numerous bars, clubs, and beach parties.
  10. St. Ivan Church - A historic church located in the heart of Old Town, offering serene surroundings and beautiful frescoes.

What to Bring

  • Comfortable walking shoes for exploring Budva's hilly and cobblestone streets.
  • A swimsuit and beachwear for enjoying Budva's beautiful beaches.
  • A sunhat, sunglasses, and sunscreen to protect against the Mediterranean sun.
  • Light, breathable clothing suitable for warm weather.
  • Camera or smartphone for capturing Budva's picturesque landscapes and historic sites.
  • Guidebook or travel app for additional information on attractions and local tips.
  • Reusable water bottle to stay hydrated during your explorations.
  • Light jacket or sweater for cooler evenings by the sea.
  • Portable charger for your electronic devices.
  • Personal medications and basic first aid essentials.

Best Time to Visit

The best time to visit Budva is during the late spring (May to June) and early autumn (September to October) when the weather is pleasant, and the tourist crowds are thinner. Summer (July to August) is peak season, offering vibrant nightlife and numerous beach events, but expect larger crowds and higher prices.

Fun Facts

  • Budva is often referred to as the "Montenegrin Miami" due to its lively beaches and bustling nightlife.
  • The Budva Riviera stretches for about 35 kilometers along the Adriatic coast, featuring numerous beaches and resorts.
  • Budva hosts the annual Sea Dance Festival, one of the biggest open-air electronic music festivals in Southeastern Europe.
  • The Old Town of Budva was largely untouched by the Yugoslav Wars, preserving its medieval architecture.
  • Budva is twinned with several cities, including Banjaluka (Bosnia and Herzegovina) and Varna (Bulgaria).
  • Famous international celebrities have visited Budva, attracted by its stunning scenery and vibrant atmosphere.
  • The Budva Municipality covers not just the town itself but also several surrounding villages and beaches.
  • Budva's name is derived from the Slavic word "budva," meaning "alert" or "vigilant," reflecting its historical significance as a fortified town.
  • The city is a popular filming location for various movies and television series due to its picturesque settings.
  • Budva has a rich culinary scene, offering a blend of Mediterranean and Balkan cuisines with fresh seafood as a highlight.
