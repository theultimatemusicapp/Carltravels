CROATIAN CITIZENSHIP JOURNEY

🇭🇷 My Path to Croatian Citizenship via My Grandfather

If you’re lucky enough to have Croatian heritage, you might be eligible to claim Croatian citizenship by descent—just like I did through my grandfather, Jakov Tomic. In this guide, I’ll walk you through my personal journey, the documents I needed, what to expect, and some hard-won lessons to help you succeed.

Why I Applied: Connecting it through your grandparent and by descent

My grandfather, Jakov Tomic, was born on the stunning island of Korčula, a gem along Croatia’s Dalmatian coast. Like many Croatians of his generation, he left the country, but our family’s connection to Croatia remained strong. Years later, I realized that his heritage could unlock Croatian citizenship for me—a second passport, access to the EU, and a deeper tie to my roots. This journey wasn’t just about paperwork; it was about honoring my family’s history.

🏛️ Who Can Apply for Croatian Citizenship by Descent?

Under Croatian law, you may be eligible if you meet these criteria:

  • You have a parent, grandparent, or great-grandparent who was a Croatian citizen at birth.
  • You can prove your lineage with official documents like birth certificates.
  • Your Croatian ancestor’s citizenship wasn’t forfeited through naturalization in another country (some exceptions apply).

I applied through my grandfather, Jakov Tomic, which is a common route, though the process requires careful attention to detail.

📋 Documents I Needed

Here’s what I had to provide for my application:

  • My grandfather’s birth certificate: Sourced from Croatia, proving his citizenship.
  • His marriage certificate: If applicable, to connect the family line.
  • My parent’s birth certificate: To establish the lineage to me.
  • My birth certificate: Apostilled and translated into Croatian.
  • Proof of my citizenship: A copy of my passport or national ID.
  • Police clearance certificate: A background check, issued within the last 6 months, apostilled and translated.
  • Application form: Obtained from the Croatian consulate, filled out in Croatian.
  • Letter of intent: A statement explaining my desire to reconnect with my Croatian heritage.

Note: Every consulate may have slightly different requirements, so always check their official checklist.

📍 Where and How to Apply

You can apply at a Croatian consulate in your country of residence or directly in Croatia through the Ministry of the Interior. I chose the consulate route—it’s slower but more straightforward for those living abroad.

Expect to:

  • Book an appointment well in advance (some consulates are booked out for months).
  • Submit notarized and translated documents into Croatian by a certified court translator NATI (sudski tumač).
  • Pay a fee, which varies by location (typically €200–€400 abroad).

⏳ How Long Does It Take?

The timeline varies depending on where you apply:

  • Outside Croatia: 12–24 months, sometimes longer due to consulate backlogs.

My application is still in progress, and I’ve learned that Croatian bureaucracy moves slowly. Patience is key, but the reward is worth it.

🌟 Why It’s Worth It

Beyond the emotional connection to my grandfather’s homeland, Croatian citizenship opens incredible opportunities:

  • Live and work anywhere in the European Union.
  • Visa-free travel to over 180 countries with a strong Croatian passport.
  • Access to EU healthcare, education, and work rights.
  • A “Plan B” passport for global mobility in an uncertain world.

🧠 My Tips for Success

Lessons I learned the hard way:

  • Start early: Tracking down Croatian documents, like my grandfather’s birth certificate, can take months.
  • Triple-check translations: Errors in translations can cause significant delays.
  • Join expat forums: Reddit, Facebook groups, and embassy pages offer valuable insights from others in the process.
  • Consider professional help: Services like Croatian by Descent can handle paperwork, translations, and filing, saving you time and stress.
