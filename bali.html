Bali

History

Bali, often referred to as the "Island of the Gods," has a history rich in art, culture, and spirituality. The island has been influenced by various cultures, including Hinduism, which plays a significant role in its traditions and ceremonies.

Population

As of 2023, Bali has a population of approximately 4.3 million people, with the majority residing in the southern regions catering to tourism.

Notable Attractions

  • Ubud Monkey Forest
  • Tanah Lot Temple
  • Seminyak Beach
  • Uluwatu Temple
  • Tegallalang Rice Terraces

Best Time to Visit

The best time to visit Bali is during the dry season, from April to October, when the weather is sunny and ideal for outdoor activities.

Prices Overview

Bali offers a range of accommodation options. Budget travelers can find hostels and guesthouses starting at USD 10-20 per night, while mid-range hotels range from USD 50-150 per night. Dining out is affordable, with meals costing between USD 5 and USD 25.

Fun Facts

  • Bali is the only Hindu-majority region in Indonesia, which is predominantly Muslim.
  • The island is home to Mount Agung, Bali's highest and most sacred volcano.
  • Bali hosts the world-famous Bali Arts Festival, celebrating the island's rich cultural heritage.
