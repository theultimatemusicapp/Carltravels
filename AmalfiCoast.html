7-DAY AMALFI COAST ITINERARY

Cliffs, Islands & Ancient Ruins

How to Get to the Amalfi Coast from Naples

Take the Circumvesuviana Train from Napoli Garibaldi Station to Sorrento. It costs around €4–€5 one way and takes about 70–75 minutes. Trains leave roughly every 30 minutes.

Pro tip: Sit on the left side of the train heading south for the best coastal views.

From Sorrento, access other Amalfi towns by ferry, bus, or private transfer.

Where to Stay

  • Sorrento – Great base with easy transport links, shops, and a lively vibe.
  • Positano – Iconic, romantic, but pricier and touristy. Perfect for couples.
  • Vico Equense – Quiet, authentic, and budget-friendly with great pizza and coastal views.

Expect to pay €70–€150 per night for mid-range stays in Sorrento or Vico Equense. Positano is more expensive in peak season.

Day 1 – Arrival in Naples & Train to the Coast

Fly into Naples International Airport, take a taxi or bus to Napoli Centrale, and board the Circumvesuviana Train to your chosen town. Check in, grab an Aperol Spritz, and enjoy the Mediterranean air.

Day 2 – Explore Sorrento

Walk along Marina Grande, eat lemon gelato, and enjoy clifftop views in Sorrento. Shop for handmade sandals and limoncello in this charming, lively town.

Dining Tip: Try La Lanterna for seafood pasta or gelato at Gelateria Primavera.

Day 3 – Ferry to Capri Island

Take a ferry from Sorrento (€20–€25 each way, 25–30 mins) to Capri. Visit the Blue Grotto (weather permitting), ride the chairlift to Mount Solaro, explore Anacapri, and sip a spritz at the Piazzetta.

Ferries run every 30–60 minutes. Book in advance during high season.

Day 4 – Visit Pompeii

Take the Circumvesuviana Train to Pompei Scavi (30–40 mins from Sorrento). Entry is ~€16, and you’ll need 3–4 hours. Bring water and wear proper shoes.

Optional: Combine with a Mount Vesuvius tour for history buffs.

Day 5 – Chill Day in Vico Equense

Relax in Vico Equense, one train stop before Sorrento. Enjoy cliff walks, local bakeries, and pizza by the metre at Pizza a Metro.

Dining Tip: Don’t miss Pizza a Metro for a unique dining experience.

Day 6 – Explore Positano or Amalfi Town

Take a SITA bus (€2–€3) or ferry (€8–€15) from Sorrento to Positano (colourful cliffs, boutiques) or Amalfi (historic cathedral, calmer vibe).

The ferry offers stunning coastal views.

Day 7 – Sunset Views & Departure

Soak up the sun, enjoy one last gelato, or catch golden hour over the cliffs. Take the train back to Naples for your flight home.

Tips for the Amalfi Coast

  • No cars: Avoid renting a car due to narrow roads and parking issues. Use trains, ferries, and buses.
  • Ferry tickets: Book in advance for Capri during high season.
  • Footwear: Wear sturdy shoes for cobblestones and steep stairs.
  • Seasonal Tip: Visit in spring (April–May) or fall (September–October) for fewer crowds and milder weather.
  • Scams: Beware of taxi overcharges in Naples; use fixed-rate taxis or buses.

Final Thoughts

The Amalfi Coast is a dream you can walk through. Sip limoncello in quiet villages, ride ferries past cinematic cliffs, or wander Pompeii’s ancient ruins. For a slice of authentic Italy, don’t skip Vico Equense.
