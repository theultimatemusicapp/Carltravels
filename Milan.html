Milan

What to See

Milan is a hub of art, fashion, and history. Key sights include the Milan Cathedral (Duomo di Milano), Galleria Vittorio Emanuele II, Sforza Castle, La Scala Opera House, and Leonardo da Vinci's "The Last Supper".

A Brief History of the City

Founded as a Celtic settlement, Milan became a major center during the Roman Empire. Throughout the centuries, it has been a focal point for art, commerce, and politics in Italy. Today, Milan seamlessly blends its rich historical heritage with modern advancements.

Best Time to Visit

Spring (April to June) and Autumn (September to October) offer pleasant weather and fewer tourists. Summer is bustling with events but can be crowded, while winter is ideal for shopping and enjoying Milan's festive atmosphere.

Fun Facts

  • Milan is considered one of the world's fashion capitals, hosting major fashion weeks twice a year.
  • The Galleria Vittorio Emanuele II is one of the world's oldest shopping malls.
  • Milan was the birthplace of Leonardo da Vinci's masterpiece, "The Last Supper".
  • The city has more apartments than people, making it one of the most densely populated cities in Europe.
  • Milan's San Siro Stadium is one of the most famous football stadiums, home to both AC Milan and Inter Milan.
