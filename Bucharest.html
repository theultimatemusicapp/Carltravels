Bucharest

What to See

Bucharest, often referred to as "Little Paris," boasts an impressive mix of historical and modern architecture. Key sights include the Palace of the Parliament, Romanian Athenaeum, Old Town (Lipscani), Herastrau Park, and the National Museum of Art of Romania.

A Brief History of the City

Bucharest's history dates back to the 14th century. It became the capital of Romania in 1862 and experienced significant growth and modernization in the 20th century. The city endured extensive damage during World War II but has since been rebuilt, blending its rich history with contemporary developments.

What to Bring

  • Comfortable walking shoes
  • Weather-appropriate clothing (Bucharest has a continental climate)
  • Camera or smartphone for photos
  • Guidebook or map
  • Travel adapter (Type F socket)
  • Reusable water bottle
  • Personal medications
  • Sun protection (sunscreen, hat, sunglasses)
  • Light jacket for cooler evenings
  • Basic Romanian phrases

Currency

The currency in Bucharest (Romania) is the Romanian Leu (RON).

Best Time to Visit

Spring (April to June) and Autumn (September to October) offer mild weather and fewer tourists. Summer is ideal for outdoor activities and exploring parks, while winter is perfect for enjoying Christmas markets and cozy cafés.

Fun Facts

  • Bucharest is home to the world's second-largest administrative building, the Palace of the Parliament.
  • The Romanian Athenaeum is renowned for its stunning architecture and is the home of the George Enescu Philharmonic Orchestra.
  • Bucharest was nicknamed "Little Paris" in the early 20th century due to its elegant architecture and vibrant cultural scene.
  • The Village Museum showcases traditional Romanian village life with over 300 authentic buildings from across the country.
  • Revolution Square was the focal point of the 1989 Romanian Revolution that led to the fall of Nicolae Ceaușescu.
