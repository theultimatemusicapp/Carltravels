Copenhagen

What to See

Copenhagen is famous for its historic sites and modern attractions. Must-visit places include Tivoli Gardens, Nyhavn Harbor, The Little Mermaid statue, Christiansborg Palace, and the vibrant Freetown Christiania.

A Brief History of the City

Founded in the 10th century, Copenhagen has grown from a small fishing village into a major European capital. It has played a crucial role in Danish history, culture, and commerce, and today it is known for its high quality of life and innovative design.

What to Bring

  • Comfortable walking shoes
  • Weather-appropriate clothing
  • Camera or smartphone for photos
  • Guidebook or map
  • Travel adapter (Type C or Type E socket)
  • Reusable water bottle
  • Personal medications
  • Light jacket for cooler evenings
  • Umbrella or raincoat
  • Basic Danish phrases

Currency

The currency in Copenhagen is the Danish Krone (DKK).

Best Time to Visit

Spring (April to June) and Autumn (September to October) offer mild weather and fewer tourists. Summer is vibrant with outdoor activities and festivals, while winter is perfect for enjoying Copenhagen's cozy hygge atmosphere and festive Christmas markets.

Fun Facts

  • Copenhagen is home to the world's first bicycle superhighway, promoting eco-friendly transportation.
  • The city consistently ranks among the world's most livable cities.
  • Nyhavn was once a busy commercial port but is now a picturesque tourist area with colorful buildings and restaurants.
  • The Little Mermaid statue is inspired by Hans Christian Andersen's fairy tale.
  • Copenhagen hosts the annual Copenhagen Jazz Festival, attracting music lovers from around the globe.
