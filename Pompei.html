Pompei

What to See

Pompei offers a captivating glimpse into ancient Roman life. Key attractions include the Forum, Villa of the Mysteries, Amphitheater, Stabian Baths, and the Lupanar (ancient brothel).

A Brief History of the City

Pompei was a thriving Roman city until the catastrophic eruption of Mount Vesuvius in AD 79. The volcanic ash preserved the city, capturing details of daily life, architecture, and art from the era.

Best Time to Visit

Spring (April to June) and Autumn (September to October) offer pleasant weather and fewer tourists. Summer is ideal for exploring outdoor sites, while winter is mild and perfect for enjoying the archaeological parks.

Fun Facts

  • Pompei is one of the most extensive and well-preserved ancient Roman cities in the world.
  • The city's eruption was recorded by Pliny the Younger, providing valuable historical accounts.
  • Pompei's frescoes and mosaics offer detailed insights into Roman art and daily life.
  • The House of the Faun contains one of the largest mosaics in Pompei, depicting the Battle of Issus.
  • Pompei was rediscovered in the 18th century, leading to significant archaeological excavations.
