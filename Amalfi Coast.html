Amalfi Coast

History

The Amalfi Coast, located in the Campania region of Italy, has a history dating back to ancient times. It was once a maritime republic alongside Venice, Genoa, and Pisa, playing a significant role in Mediterranean trade. The picturesque coastline has inspired artists, writers, and travelers for centuries.

Population

As of 2023, the Amalfi Coast region has a population of approximately 180,000 people spread across its various towns and villages.

Notable Attractions

  • Positano
  • Amalfi
  • Ravello
  • Villa Cimbrone
  • Path of the Gods

Best Time to Visit

The best time to visit the Amalfi Coast is during the late spring (May and June) and early autumn (September and October) when the weather is warm, and the tourist crowds are thinner.

Prices Overview

The Amalfi Coast can be expensive, especially during peak seasons. Accommodation ranges from budget hotels at €70 per night to luxury resorts exceeding €300 per night. Dining out costs between €20 for casual meals to €60+ at upscale restaurants.

Fun Facts

  • The Amalfi Coast is a UNESCO World Heritage Site recognized for its stunning landscapes and historical significance.
  • Positano, one of the main towns, is known for its steep streets and colorful buildings cascading down the cliffs.
  • The region is famous for producing Limoncello, a popular Italian lemon liqueur.
